MY STORY

It all started with one game I happened to play in middle school on the PSP. The game was Final Fantasy VII: Crisis Core and upon beating it, I couldn't stop thinking about it. I kept thinking back to the story it told and the unforgettable experience it gave me.

When the time came to pick a high school, I chose A-Tech since it had a program for Computer Science. I learned Visual Basic as a freshman, Java for both sophomore and junior year, and got to make my own project as a senior. As a junior, I took a web development class and a graphic design class. I started thinking that I had an interest in web development or game development around that time as well. When senior year came around, I made a JRPG in C#/XNA for five months with two of my best friends just for fun. We showed it off at our school's Employer's Day and it was a hit.

From that point on, I knew I wanted to continue studies in Computer Science and try making games along the way. The B.S. in Computer Science (Games) at USC seemed like the perfect fit and it was also fairly close to Las Vegas where I lived. Now I live in Los Angeles, studying at USC as a sophomore, still pursuing my passion for making (and playing) games.
